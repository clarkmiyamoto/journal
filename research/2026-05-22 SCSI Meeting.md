# Setup for CryoEM (Meeting notes Jiequn Han)
In CryoEM, the biologist makes a micrograph
![[2026-05-19 micrograph.png]]
and images it, this defines a dataset of a thousand to million photos of 2D projections of 3D protein(s). They want to do the reconstruction given a micrograph.

**Types of problem**
- **Homogeneity**: Dataset is composed the same single molecule. $\pi = \delta_x$ 
- **Discrete heterogeneity:** Dataset is composed of $K$ different molecules. $\pi = \frac{1}{K}\sum_{i=1}^K \delta_{x_i}$ 
	- You may or may not be given knowledge that there are $K$ different molecules. Dataset will be unlabeled (e.g. you won't know which image came from which molecule).
- **Continuous heterogeneity:** Each projection image in the dataset comes from a realization from a continuous distribution over molecules. Meaning each image in the dataset is generated by, $y = \mathcal F(x)$ s.t. $x \overset{\text{iid}}{\sim} \pi$  where $\pi$ is more expressive than a delta function (e.g. Gaussian mixture).
	- This could be the same protein, but with slight deformations.
	- This could be multiple proteins, all with slight deformations.

**Evaluation**
- Discrete heterogeneity: Use generative model to sample $\pi$, get samples divvy them up into classes (perhaps use a classifier here). Per class, apply evaluation methods used on the homogeneous problem.
- Continuous heterogeneity: it's not clear how to make a quantitative evaluation of this problem
> Compare to other reconstruction methods. PDB (protein databank)

**Dataset size**
- 1k-1M photos

**Corruption channel / model misspecification**
- Rotations
	- Depends on geometry of molecule
	- Depends on tightness of packing
- Noise level
	- Can be effected by molecule
	- However, in simple case, it is believed the noise level is well estimated.
- TEM
	- Too strong of electron beam can alter proteins
	- CTF estimation. Miscalibration (or lens distortions) in the camera can cause ripple effects in image.
