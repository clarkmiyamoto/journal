# Notes
Consider a clean data distribution $P^*_X$ in space $\mathcal X$, and a likelihood $Q(\cdot | X)$ which maps $X \in \mathcal X$ to a distribution in corrupted (observed) space $\mathcal Y$
$$
\begin{align}
Y \sim Q(\cdot |X) , \text{ s.t. } X \sim P_X^*
\end{align}
$$
Let $P^*$ be the joint over $(X,Y)$, and let $P_Y^*$ is the marginal of $Y$.

Let $\mathcal F :  \mathcal X \to \mathbb R^d$ be the forward operator (corruption channel). 

**Setup** You have access to a dataset of corrupted samples $\left\{ Y^{[i]} \right\}_i$ from $P_Y^*$ and $Q$ is known.  

**Goal**
- **Unconditional generation** Generate new samples from ground truth data distribtuion $P_X^*$
- **Posterior sampling** Sample $X \sim P^*(\cdot | Y)$ given an observation $Y$

## EM Approach
Consider a class of parameterized latent variable model $\left\{ q_\theta(x,y) \right\}_\theta$ which aims to represent the ground truth distribution over $(X,Y)$. Let $q_\theta(y)$ be the probability density of the marginal distribution of $Y$.

EM seeks a parameter $\theta$ that maximizes the population log-likelihood of observable variables
$$
\begin{align}
\max_\theta \mathcal L(\theta) := \mathbb E_{Y \sim P_Y^*} \log q_\theta(Y)
\end{align}
$$
This optimization problem is equivalent to minimizing the KL divergence between $P_Y^*$ and $q_\theta(y)$. 
$$
\begin{align}
\mathcal L(\theta) \geq \mathbb E_{Y \sim P_Y^*} \mathbb E_{X \sim q_{\hat \theta}(X|Y)} \log \frac{q_\theta(X,Y)}{q_{\hat \theta}(X|Y)}
\end{align}
$$
In particular, we start at an initial point $\theta^{(0)}$, and iterate
$$
\begin{align}
\theta^{(k+1)} &  = \arg \max_\theta \mathbb E_{Y \sim P_Y^*} \mathbb E_{X \sim q_{\theta^{(k)}}(X|Y)} \log q_\theta(X,Y)\\
\end{align}
$$
**EM with conditional diffusion model**
In the $E$-step, generate the dataset $\mathcal D_X^{(k)} \sim q_{\theta^{(k)}}$ by using the diffusion model. In the $M$ step use $\mathcal D_X^{(k)}$ to train $q_{\theta^{(k+1)}}$ according to
$$
\begin{align}
\mathcal L_{SM,k}(\theta) = \int_0^1 \lambda_t \mathbb E_{X \sim \mathcal D_{X^{(k)}}, Y \sim Q(\cdot | X)} \mathbb E_{X_t = X + \sigma_t Z} \|s_\theta(X_t, t | Y) + Z\|^2 \, dt
\end{align}
$$
**Algorithm**
```pseudo
\begin{algorithm}
\begin{algorithmic}
\Require Corrupted observations $\mathcal D = \left\{ Y_i \right\}_i$ and likelihood $Q(\cdot | X)$ , and initialization for conditional model $\theta^{(0)}$.
\For{$k = 0,1,..., K-1$}
	\For{$i \in [N]$}
		\State $X_i \sim q_{\theta^{(k)}}(\cdot | Y_i)$
		\State Train new conditional diffusion model using $\mathcal L{SM, k}(\theta)$
    \EndFor
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
```
