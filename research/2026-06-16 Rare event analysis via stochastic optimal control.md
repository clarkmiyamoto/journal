Problem: Rare event are rare transitions between meta stablestates. E.g. nucleation, crystallization. We want to compute the reaction rate, transition pathways, and the transition mechanism.

How rare? For example in molecular dynamics, this might be $10^9 - 10^{15}$ for one rare event.

**Definition (Committor Function)**. 
Given dynamics $dX_t = b(X_t) dt + \sigma dW_t$
$$
\begin{align}
q(x) = \mathbb P_x(X_\tau \in B), \quad \tau = \inf\left\{ t \geq 0 : X_t \in A \cup B \right\}
\end{align}
$$
The $\mathbb P_x$ denotes starting the trajectory at $x$; it is the fraction that hit $B$ before $A$ estimates $q(x)$.
- $q \approx 0$: likely hits $A$
- $q \approx 1$: likely hits $B$
- $q = 0.5$: stochastic separatrix

Since this is a Markov process, we can write it as a Markov semigroup operator
$$
\begin{align}
q(x) = \mathbb E_x[q(X_{\Delta t})] = P_{\Delta t} \, q(x)
\end{align}
$$
Now consider the infinitesimal generator
$$
\begin{align}
\mathcal L f = \lim_{t \downarrow 0} \frac{P_t f -f}{t}
\end{align}
$$
So we can now derive a Fokker Planck generated by the committer semigroup. I'll denote these pdf's as $q$ and $\tilde q$

Transition path theory then gives you nice information

However you don't get this for free. To learn the committor you need to be near a $A,B$ space (these are the points of interest in the committer function).

## Method
One method is called Variational BKE,
- Issue requires $\mathcal L$ to be self-adjoint

Another method is called BKE-IS / Biased Potential Sampling
- Issue: The committor biased potential can over concentrate samples near the guessed transition region in practice.

Another method is called Short-Trajectory Semigroup Matching
Use Markov semigroup fixed point $q = P_\tau q$
$$
\begin{align}
\min_{\hat q} = \mathbb E_{x_0 \sim \mu_0} \left[ |\hat q(X_0) - \mathbb E[\hat q(X_\tau ) | X_0] |^2\right]
\end{align}
$$
where $x_0$ is initialized near $A$.
- Issues: If rollouts are unbiased, most trajectories remain in a basin. Learn $\hat q$ doesn't improve later rollout sampling.
