#stochasticprocess 

**Lemma**
Consider a true state $X_t$ evolving under the SDE
$$
\begin{align}
dX_t = b_t(X_t)dt + \sigma_t  dW_t
\end{align}
$$
where $b_t$ is one-sided Lipschitz in the position. That is $\exists \lambda > 0$ such that $\forall x,y$ 
$$
\begin{align}
\langle x - y, b_t(x) - b_t(y) \rangle \leq - \lambda \|x-y\|^2
\end{align}
$$
Now consider an approximate state $Y_t$ generated by an approximate drift with the same RNG seed (the $dW_t$'s are the same)
$$
\begin{align}
dY_t = \hat b_t(Y_t)  dt + \sigma_t dW_t
\end{align}
$$
where the error on the drift is uniformly bounded 
$$
\begin{align}
\sup_x \| b_t(x) - \hat b_t(x) \| \leq \Delta
\end{align}
$$

If $X_0 = Y_0$, then the distance between the exact trajectory and the approximate trajectory is bounded for all time $t \geq 0$ by
$$
\begin{align}
	\|X_t - Y_t \| \leq \frac \Delta \lambda (1- e^{-\lambda t}) \leq \frac \Delta \lambda
\end{align}
$$
____
*Proof*

Consider the difference between the two processes $Z_t := X_t - Y_t$
$$
\begin{align}
dZ_t & = (b_t(X_t) - \hat b(Y_t)) dt + (\sigma_t dW_t - \sigma dW_t)\\
& = (b_t(X_t) - \hat b_t(Y_t)) dt
\end{align}
$$
Now consider how its norm time evolves
$$
\begin{align}
	\frac{1}{2}\frac{d}{dt} \|Z_t\|^2 & = \langle Z_t, \frac{dZ_t}{dt}\rangle\\
	& = \langle X_t - Y_t, b_t(X_t) - \hat b_t(Y_t) \rangle\\
	& = \langle X_t - Y_t, b_t(X_t) - b_t(Y_t) \rangle + \langle X_t  - Y_t, b_t(Y_t) - \hat b_t(Y_t)\rangle
\end{align}
$$
The first term is bounded by the one-sided Lipschitz. The second term is bounded by Cauchy Schwarz $\langle X_t - Y_t , b_t(Y_t) - \hat b_t(Y_t) \rangle \leq  \|Z_t\| \cdot \|b_t(Y_t) - \hat b_t(Y_t)\|$ and the drift is uniformly bounded $\|b_t - \hat b_t\| \leq \Delta$ 
$$
\begin{align}
	\frac{1}{2} \frac{d}{dt} \|Z_t\|^2 & = \|Z_t\| \frac{d \|Z_t\|}{dt} \leq - \lambda \|Z_t\|^2 + \Delta \|Z_t\|\\
	 \frac{d \|Z_t\|}{dt} & \leq - \lambda \|Z_t\| + \Delta
\end{align}
$$
Now you can use Gromwalls inequality
$$
\begin{align}
\|Z_t\| \leq \frac{\Delta}{\lambda} (1 - e^{- \lambda t}) \leq \frac{\Delta}{ \lambda}
\end{align}
$$

### Discussion
We can tighten this bound if you can analytically solve
$$
\begin{align}
dZ_t = (b_t(X_t) - \hat b_t(Y_t)) dt
\end{align}
$$
